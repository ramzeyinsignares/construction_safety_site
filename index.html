Construction Safety: A Persistent Problem

Construction remains one of the most hazardous industries in the United States. Despite engineering advances and modern project delivery methods, thousands of workers are injured or killed each year in incidents that are often preventable.

According to national safety data, construction consistently accounts for a disproportionate share of workplace fatalities. These incidents impact not only workers, but also families, communities, and industries that rely on safe and efficient infrastructure development. The core problem is clear: construction workers continue to face unacceptable levels of risk, and many current safety practices have not kept pace with the complexity of modern job sites.

Key Issue

The Human Cost of Inaction

Falls, struck-by incidents, caught-in/between events, and electrocutions remain leading causes of death on construction sites. Each statistic represents a person whose life and livelihood was impacted by conditions that are, in many cases, preventable.

A Dual Approach to Safer Construction

Improving worker safety requires a combined focus on emerging safety technologies and strong, enforceable safety policies. Technology alone cannot solve structural problems, and policy alone cannot monitor every hazard in real time. Together, they offer a path toward proactive, data-informed safety management.

How You Can Support Safer Construction

Construction safety affects workers, families, and communities. By staying informed and supporting policies and practices that prioritize worker well-being, members of the public can help shape a safer built environment.
